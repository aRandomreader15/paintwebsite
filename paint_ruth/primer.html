Primer

It's highly recommended that you use primer before painting, especially if you have fresh, unpainted drywall. Fresh drywall loves to suck up paint if not primed, so if you want to avoid losing multiple coats of paint to the gluttonous drywall, prime.

Most paint cans say that they contain paint + primer. They are lying to you! Do not listen to them! It's not possible for there to be paint and primer in the can at the same time without it doing a subpar job at priming. The primer is trying to seal the wall at the same time as the paint which is trying to adhere to it. They get in eachothers way and all it does is require you to put on another coat of expensive paint when you could've just primed. Using the paint with the assumption that the "primer" will work is like all season tires or 2 in 1 shampoo+conditioner, mediocre results at best. There really isn't any primer, which can be problematic if you have a problem with the wall that needs a specific type of primer to deal with.

Save yourself the time and effort, buy primer and don't rely on the paint itself to do the job.

Speciality primers

Certain primers are made for different jobs. If your wall is a bright neon colour and you want to put a pastel colour on it, high-hiding primer will ensure none of the original colour peaks through.

Application methods

You can use the same methods/tools for primer as you would paint. Just make sure to let the primer fully dry before putting on a second coat or starting with your paint. Refer to the can or SDS if unsure of when it's dry to the touch and cured. Curing means all of the primer is dry, including the stuff inside the walls. Something being fully cured means there shouldn't be any complications with putting on a new coat of paint but it's not the end of the world if you just wait until it's dry to the touch instead of fully cured.

Clean up!

If you plan to use your brushes in the future, make sure to wash them with warm soapy water before the paint dries. Ensure that all of the paint has been removed, even the stuff you can't immediately see. If you get paint on a surface that you don't want paint to be on, you can use paint remover or scrape it when it dries.
