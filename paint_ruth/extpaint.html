Exterior Paint

Exterior paint is the thicker, more durable, version of interior paint. You can use it on brick walls, cement, concrete, wood, and more.

Finishes

Exterior paint has less selections of finishes when it comes to its paint. That's because it only carries Flat, Satin, and Semi-Gloss. Whenever you're painting something outside where the elements can get to it, prioritizing a finish that will help aid in the durability of your paint job is key.

Application methods

You can use multiple different tools to apply paint. You can use a brush, roller and even a sprayer to put paint on your walls. Most surfaces that require exterior paint will require a thicker nap for a roller. The thicker the nap, Make sure your paint is fully dry before putting on a second coat. Check the side of the can or the SDS online if you're unsure of dry times, clean up methods, and more. Avoid using exterior paint inside as it contains a higher amount of fumes that aren't great for humans to inhale/spend a long time around. It might be tempting due to it being more durable but its safer to just not.

Coats

Make sure you use enough coats on your walls (or other surfaces) to ensure that the paint does not peel but rather holds up to the wear and tear of the weather. See the side of the can for specifics but two coats is always recommended.
