Colour

Colour is deceptively the hardest choice when it comes to paint. Whether it's trying to find the perfect colour to fit your walls, or wanting to match a colour with perfect accuracy, it can be hard to know where to look to find the right colour.

Finding the perfect colour

When it comes to finding colours for your house, it can be hard knowing where to start. I'd reccomend going in person if possible to the paint store and looking at their provided swatches. While you can choose colours that are not part of the brands that they sell, if you are starting from nothing it makes it easier for both you and the employee if you pick a physical colour at the store. Often times the angle or brightness of the screen can skew the actual colour if you are looking at it from your device.

If you choose to reference the colours that they have in person, make sure to angle whatever swatch you pick away from the fluorescent lights as those lights will change what the colour looks like. I'd reccomend taking whatever swatches you choose outside and to your home if you can, to properly see what it looks like in different lighting situations.

You also don't have to pick a colour that's linked to a brand. Ex: If you love a valspar colour but want sico paint, don't worry! The employee can easily make the colour work regardless of it's brand. This applies to colours that aren't part of their system. If the store only carries sico and valspar colours and paint, but you really love a behr colour, the employee can cross match that colour so long as you have the name or number of that colour.

Finding a non company colour

If you have a colour on your wall and you want more of it but you forgot the name, brand, number etc about the colour, fret not. So long as you take a clean quarter sized strip of the colour and bring it into the store, a paint employee can replicate it. So long as their scanner is functioning, they can scan your strip of paint and recreate it to a 99% accuracy if not more.

Changing colours

If a mistake is made while tinting or you really dislike the colour you chose, we can change it (sometimes). So long as we have the mistinted colour, and a physical copy of the colour you want, we can change the colour by scanning both. Please note that this only works if you are changing a colour from light to dark. We cannot make things lighter, only darker.

Disclaimer!

Some of these techniques mentioned above require a semi-thorough knowledge of paint and the system that the store runs on. Please be kind and understanding to the paint associates at the store if the associates don't know some of the techniques above. Training doesn't always cover these things and newer associates might not have had the experience needed to know how to work things. Also be understanding that sometimes our equipment bricks itself and we can't scan things or crossmatch colours because of it. Come back another week or check elsewhere but please don't take out your frustrations on the employees, they likely called IT already and turned off and on the system. Don't be mean, but rather be patient, and they'll likely work with you to find a solution despite the tech problems!
